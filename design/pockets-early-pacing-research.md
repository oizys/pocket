# First-5-Minutes Pacing Research — for Pockets

Cross-game survey of how survival/crafting/exploration games open. Goal: inform Pockets' starter content size and the explore-vs-craft ratio.

## Minecraft (Java, Survival, fresh world)

### Verbs and targets (first 5 min)
- **Walk / look** around the spawn biome
- **Punch (hold left-click) tree** → wood log drops
- **Pick up** floating log items (automatic on proximity)
- **Open inventory** (E)
- **Craft** in the 2x2 personal grid
- **Place** crafting table on the ground
- **Craft** at the 3x3 station
- **Mine stone** with the pickaxe once made

### First 3–5 recipes
1. 1 log → 4 planks (personal grid)
2. 4 planks → 1 crafting table (personal grid)
3. 2 planks stacked vertically → 4 sticks (at table)
4. 3 planks + 2 sticks → 1 wooden pickaxe (at table)
5. 3 planks + 2 sticks → 1 wooden axe, or 2 planks + 1 stick → 1 wooden sword (player choice)

### Functional narrative
There is no tutorial popup in vanilla. Knowledge is environmental and assumed. Order experienced players follow (and what the wiki "Your first 10 minutes" tutorial teaches): **see hand → punch tree → see inventory fill → open inventory and notice the 2x2 grid → craft planks → craft table → place table → craft tools → start mining → notice hunger bar dropping after ~5 min**. Day/night is implicit (sun position). No quests, no NPCs.

### Inventory at 5 min
~3–6 stacks, ~3–5 unique types: logs (often 1 stack), planks, sticks, crafting table (placed), wooden pickaxe, maybe sapling/apple drops.

### Ceiling (single-minded farming, 5 min)
~6–10 distinct types: logs, planks, sticks, saplings, apples (oak), flowers, dirt, leaves (with shears, not applicable), and 1–2 tools.

### Craft vs explore
**~75–80% gather/explore, ~20–25% craft.** Crafting is brief because recipes are tiny and the grid is in your pocket.

## Stardew Valley (fresh save, day 1)

### Verbs (first 5 min)
- **Read** Grandpa's letter cutscene
- **Walk** out of the farmhouse
- **Talk** to Mayor Lewis and Robin
- **Pick up** the parsnip seeds on the floor
- **Equip** tool from hotbar (axe, hoe, pickaxe, scythe, watering can — all gifted)
- **Chop** weeds/twigs, **break** small stones with pickaxe
- **Till** soil (hoe), **plant** seed, **water** with can

### First 3–5 recipes
Day 1 has effectively **zero crafting**. Crafting is gated by skill levels. You may see one "recipe" via tilling+planting (15 Parsnip Seeds → planted parsnips after Lewis gifts them). The first true crafted item most players make is a **Chest (50 wood)** or **Wooden Fence (2 wood)** — both unlocked by default, but usually not within the first 5 minutes because chopping a tree to stump takes ~30–60s and wood drops are 1–3 per chop.

### Functional narrative
Heavy guided opening. **Cutscene → letter from Grandpa → arrive in Pelican Town → Lewis greets you → walk you to your farm → he and Robin point out your tools and seeds → "go plant something" implicit quest pops in the journal.** Day/night is on a clock from minute one (6:00 AM start; energy bar and time-of-day are explained via tooltips on first hover).

### Inventory at 5 min
~5–7 stacks, ~6 unique types: 5 tools (axe, pickaxe, hoe, watering can, scythe) + 15 parsnip seeds + a few wood/fiber from clearing.

### Ceiling (5 min)
~8–12 types: tools (5) + parsnip seeds + wood + fiber + stone + weeds/forageables (spring onion, dandelion, leek, daffodil if you sprint to the bus stop area) + maybe a worm-spot item.

### Craft vs explore
**~0% craft, ~100% talk/walk/clear.** Stardew front-loads narrative and tool familiarization; crafting is deliberately deferred.

## Dwarf Fortress — **Fortress Mode** (chosen over Adventure)

**Why Fortress Mode**: Pockets is described as inventory + crafting + facilities. Adventure mode is closer to a roguelike. Fortress mode's wagon-unload → stockpile → workshop loop is the better analogue.

### Verbs (first 5 min)
The player issues *orders*; dwarves execute. Player verbs:
- **Pause** (immediately, by convention)
- **Pan camera / scroll z-levels**
- **Designate** trees for chopping
- **Designate** mining tiles
- **Build** stockpile zones
- **Build** workshops (Carpenter, Mason)
- **Set** labors (in Classic) / manage work orders (in Premium/Steam)

### First 3–5 recipes
1. Logs → Wooden Bed (Carpenter's workshop, 1 log)
2. Logs → Wooden Door (1 log)
3. Stone → Stone Door / Block (Mason's workshop, 1 stone)
4. Logs → Barrels (1 log → 1 barrel, for food/drink storage)
5. Plant gathering → brewing at Still (5 plants → 5 drinks in a barrel)

### Functional narrative
**Embark screen (squad of 7, skills, supplies) → arrive at site with wagon → pause → survey terrain → designate trees → designate dig site → build carpenter → build mason → build still → build farm plot.** Steam version (2022+) has a tutorial overlay; Classic has nothing. Hunger/thirst/sleep/mood are simulated but invisible to first-time players until a dwarf complains.

### Inventory at 5 min
The wagon arrives with a *fixed embark inventory* (default ~30–50 items: picks, axes, anvil, seeds, drinks, food, cloth, etc.). So **5-min "inventory" is essentially the embark loadout**, ~20–40 unique types, ~30–60 stacks. The player has gathered nothing yet — they're still designating.

### Ceiling (5 min)
Effectively still the embark loadout plus maybe 1–2 logs chopped and 0–5 stones mined.

### Craft vs explore
**~10% craft (designating workshops), ~50% plan/place (zones, stockpiles), ~40% camera explore.** Inverted compared to action-survival because the player is a manager, not an avatar.

## Rune Factory 5 (most-played recent entry)

### Verbs (first 5 min)
- **Walk** (linear corridor through opening forest)
- **Talk** to NPC (Livia in distress)
- **Attack** monsters (forced combat tutorial)
- **Watch cutscene** (collapse at gate, wake in clinic)
- **Read** dialogue boxes

### First 3–5 recipes
**Zero in the first 5 minutes.** Crafting (forging, cooking, chemistry, accessories) is gated behind owning the relevant table, which comes hours later. The first "recipe-like" action is **farming**: hoe → till → seed → water. The farming tutorial with Priscilla typically fires around the **20–40 minute mark**, not the first 5.

### Functional narrative
**Opening cinematic → amnesia premise → forced combat tutorial → cutscene → wake in clinic → meet Simone (doctor), Murakumo (innkeeper), the mayor → join SEED.** Day/night clock, RP (stamina), HP all introduced via popups during the combat tutorial. Farming and shipping box come later in the prologue.

### Inventory at 5 min
~2–4 items: starter sword (gift), maybe 1–2 monster drops from the tutorial fight.

### Ceiling (5 min)
~3–5 types — the opening corridor doesn't have foraging spawns to speak of.

### Craft vs explore
**~0% craft, ~70% cutscene/dialogue, ~30% combat-walk.** Rune Factory is the most narrative-front-loaded game in this set.

## Factorio (fresh freeplay, no scenarios)

### Verbs (first 5 min)
- **Walk** (WASD)
- **Chop trees** (left-click held)
- **Mine** stone/coal/iron/copper nodes by hand (hold left-click on ore)
- **Open inventory** (E)
- **Open crafting menu** (same E panel)
- **Place** buildings (right-click to take from inventory, left-click to place)
- **Fuel** burner devices (right-click drag wood/coal into burner)

### First 3–5 recipes
Starting inventory (vanilla freeplay): **1 burner mining drill, 1 stone furnace, some wood.**
1. Manually mine **iron ore** → **5 ore → 5 iron plates** (smelt in stone furnace, fueled by wood)
2. **2 iron plates → 1 iron gear wheel**
3. **3 iron gears + 3 iron plates + 1 stone furnace → 1 burner mining drill** (replicating the starter)
4. **5 stone → 1 stone furnace**
5. **1 iron plate → 2 iron sticks** (later) and **wood → wood-handled items** — usually a player crafts a second burner drill or a chest before 5 min is up

### Functional narrative
No tutorial in freeplay (the campaign/scenarios have one). Freeplay teaches via **inventory layout**: the recipe panel is tabbed by category, and your starter items hint at the loop. **Mine by hand → smelt → craft drill → automate one ore → repeat.** No hunger, no day/night (lighting changes but it's cosmetic at first), pollution is hidden until biters react. Map (M) is available immediately.

### Inventory at 5 min
~10–15 stacks, ~8–12 unique types: iron ore, iron plate, copper ore (if mined), copper plate, stone, coal, wood, gears, burner drill (placed/spare), stone furnace (placed/spare).

### Ceiling (5 min)
~12–18 distinct types if you sprint between resource patches: iron ore, copper ore, stone, coal, wood, iron plate, copper plate, iron gear, copper cable (2 copper → 4 cable), electronic circuit (1 iron + 3 copper cable), iron stick, plus placeables (drill, furnace, wooden chest, transport belt if you push it).

### Craft vs explore
**~50% craft, ~50% mine/walk.** Factorio is the most craft-heavy opening in this set because the loop *is* crafting from minute one — but everything you craft is gated on raw-resource gathering that happens in parallel.

## Satisfactory (fresh save)

### Verbs (first 5 min)
- **Drop pod cinematic / land**
- **Walk / sprint**
- **Punch leaves** (E on foliage) → biomass
- **Mine** iron/copper/limestone nodes by hand (hold E)
- **Open** the HUB door
- **Craft** at the HUB Craft Bench (hold and release to manufacture)
- **Build** the HUB itself (place HUB Parts assembled at the workbench)

### First 3–5 recipes (HUB Tier 0 onboarding, fixed sequence)
1. Build the HUB — 10 HUB Parts (each 4 iron plates) is the very first task
2. **HUB Upgrade 1: Iron Rod** — craft iron rods (1 iron ingot → 1 iron rod) and submit
3. **HUB Upgrade 2: Biomass Burner** — submit iron plates + biomass; unlocks first power source
4. **HUB Upgrade 3–5**: progressively introduces cable (copper wire → cable), concrete (limestone → concrete), and the constructor automation building
5. **HUB Upgrade 6 (Tier 0 capstone):** submit 100 iron rods, 100 iron plates, 100 cable, 50 concrete in storage containers (uncertain on exact numbers — confirmed "100/100/100/50" via search; recipe ingredients confirmed via wiki)

The first 5 minutes typically reach HUB Upgrade 1 or 2.

### Functional narrative
**Pod lands → ADA voiceover intro → "build the HUB" objective on-screen → mine iron by hand → smelt at hand-craft bench → assemble HUB Parts → place HUB → access milestone terminal → HUB Upgrade 1 prompts iron rods.** Explicit, voiced, on-screen objective text every step. No hunger, no day/night threat (only cosmetic). Map is locked until later.

### Inventory at 5 min
~6–10 stacks, ~5–8 unique types: iron ore, iron ingot, iron plate, iron rod, leaves/wood (biomass feedstock), limestone (if discovered nearby), HUB Parts (in the build menu, not inventory).

### Ceiling (5 min)
~10–12 types if you wander: iron ore, copper ore, limestone, leaves, wood, mycelia, flower petals, iron ingot, iron plate, iron rod, biomass, solid biofuel.

### Craft vs explore
**~40% craft (HUB bench is slow, hand-crank gating), ~50% mine, ~10% walk to find nodes.** Heaviest guided onboarding of the set.

## Moonlighter (act 1, opening Golem Dungeon)

### Verbs (first 5 min)
- **Walk** through Will's tutorial dungeon
- **Attack** (broom is the starter weapon in the prologue)
- **Pick up** artifacts that drop from enemies
- **Roll/dodge**
- **Watch** the impossible-to-win battle and forced KO
- **Talk** to Zenon, who gives you a sword + shield
- **Walk** back to the Moonlighter shop in Rynoka

### First 3–5 recipes
**Zero in the first 5 minutes.** Crafting (Forge, Witch, Tailor, Potion, Banker) is unlocked once those NPCs move to Rynoka — explicitly hours later. The first interactive "system" is **shop pricing**: drag artifacts onto pedestals and set a price.

### Functional narrative
**Cutscene of Moonlighter heritage → enter Golem Dungeon as Will → tutorial combat with broom → unwinnable swarm → KO → wake in Moonlighter shop → Zenon teaches selling → sword + shield gifted → next day, re-enter dungeon.** Dungeon → shop loop is core; both halves are introduced in act 1 prologue but most of the systems (potions, enchantments, recipes) come later.

### Inventory at 5 min
~2–4 items: broom (lost in cutscene), then sword + shield + 2–4 placeholder artifacts.

### Ceiling (5 min)
~5–8 types — Golem Dungeon Floor 1 artifact pool is small (golem-themed artifacts, gold coins, hearts).

### Craft vs explore
**~0% craft, ~80% combat/explore, ~20% dialogue.** Crafting is *deliberately* withheld to teach the shop-loop first.

## Valheim (additional pick — fits the loop perfectly)

### Verbs (first 5 min)
- **Wake up** at sacrificial stones (cinematic drop by Hugin the raven)
- **Read** Hugin's tutorial popup
- **Pick up** branches (E) and stones (E) from the ground — no tool needed
- **Punch tree** to drop wood
- **Craft** in the personal menu (Tab) — no station needed for first items
- **Eat** raspberries/mushrooms picked from bushes

### First 3–5 recipes
1. **1 wood + 1 stone → Stone Axe (hand-craft, no station)** — actually 5 wood + 4 stone per the wiki
2. **10 wood → Hammer** (gives access to the workbench build menu)
3. **10 wood → Workbench** (placed)
4. **6 wood + 6 stone → Hoe** (at workbench) — usually later
5. **8 wood + 2 leather scraps + 4 deer trophies → Club** etc.

Within 5 minutes a player typically gets the **Stone Axe and Hammer**, and starts placing a Workbench.

### Functional narrative
**Hugin tutorial popups gate concept introduction**: arrival → "pick up stones and branches" → "open inventory" → "craft a stone axe" → "find food" → "build a workbench". Hunger appears as 3 food slots from minute one. Stamina is on-screen. Day/night is real and dangerous (greydwarves come at night).

### Inventory at 5 min
~6–9 stacks, ~6–8 unique types: wood, stone, branches, raspberries, mushrooms, stone axe, hammer, maybe flint.

### Ceiling (5 min)
~10–12 types in a coastal Meadows spawn: wood, stone, flint, branches, raspberries, mushrooms, dandelion, feathers, resin (from greydwarves at dusk), stone axe, hammer, club.

### Craft vs explore
**~30% craft, ~70% pickup/walk.** Hugin pacing forces a clean explore→craft→explore rhythm.

## Terraria (additional pick — fits the loop perfectly)

### Verbs (first 5 min)
- **Walk** (A/D), **jump** (space)
- **Chop tree** with starter copper axe (left-click)
- **Mine** dirt/stone with starter copper pickaxe
- **Attack** slime with starter copper shortsword
- **Talk** to the Guide NPC (built-in tutorial helper)
- **Open inventory** and craft (mobile crafting menu shows recipes)

### First 3–5 recipes
1. **10 wood → Workbench** (hand-craft)
2. **10 wood → Wooden Sword / Wooden Bow** (at workbench)
3. **1 wood → 1 Wooden Arrow x5 + gel** (later, needs torch tier)
4. **1 wood + 1 gel → 3 Torches**
5. **75 wood → Wooden Armor set** (helmet 20 + body 35 + legs 20)

Most players get the **workbench + a wood tool or two** within 5 minutes.

### Functional narrative
**Spawn with Guide NPC + 3 starter tools → he asks "talk to me to see recipes" → chop trees → kill green slimes → build workbench → kill more slimes for gel → craft torches before nightfall.** Day/night with a 24-min cycle drives urgency (first night = Eye of Cthulhu eventually, zombies always). Health is on-screen; mana shows after collecting a fallen star.

### Inventory at 5 min
~6–10 stacks, ~6–8 types: copper pickaxe, copper axe, copper shortsword, wood, gel, dirt, stone, mushroom, acorn.

### Ceiling (5 min)
~12–15 types: above + torches, wooden sword, wooden bow, wooden arrows, workbench (placed), maybe a heart (life crystal not yet, but hearts from pots), grass seeds.

### Craft vs explore
**~25% craft, ~75% chop/mine/fight.** Closer to Minecraft than to Factorio.

## Cross-cutting synthesis

### Pacing and order of explanation — consensus
Across **8 games**, the introduction order converges to:

1. **Movement / camera** (universal, minute 0)
2. **Pick up / interact** (universal, minute 0–1) — even Dwarf Fortress's "designate" is structurally "tell something to be picked up"
3. **Inventory exists** (minute 0–2) — either auto-shown (Stardew, Satisfactory, Moonlighter) or discovered (Minecraft, Factorio, Terraria, Valheim)
4. **Hit / damage a target** (minute 1–3) — trees, ore nodes, slimes, monsters. Universal.
5. **Crafting in your hand / personal menu** (minute 2–4) — Minecraft 2x2, Valheim/Factorio recipe panel, Satisfactory HUB bench
6. **Place a facility** (minute 3–6) — crafting table, workbench, HUB, carpenter shop, stone furnace. Universal-ish; Stardew/RF5/Moonlighter defer this past 5 min.
7. **Hunger / stamina / day-night** (minute 3–10) — appears mid-tutorial in Valheim/Terraria/Minecraft; same-screen from minute 0 in Stardew/RF5; absent in Factorio/Satisfactory/Moonlighter.

**The single most consistent finding**: *every game introduces "pick up" before "craft", and "craft in your pocket" before "craft at a station".* No exceptions. Pockets should respect this.

### Number of items / facilities / recipes — typical ranges

| Bucket | Min | Median | Max | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Unique item types in bag at 5 min | 2 (RF5) | **6–8** | 20–40 (DF) | DF is an outlier because of fixed embark loadout |
| Unique items *acquirable* (5-min ceiling) | 3 (RF5) | **8–12** | 12–18 (Factorio) | |
| Crafting stations placed by 5 min | 0 (Stardew, RF5, Moonlighter) | **1** | 2 (Factorio, Satisfactory) | |
| Recipes completed/seen by 5 min | 0 (Stardew, RF5, Moonlighter) | **3–4** | 5+ (Factorio, Minecraft) | |

**Recommended starter sizing for Pockets**: **5–10 item types in the world, 1–2 stations, 2–4 recipes** by minute 5. This sits squarely in the median for action-survival-crafters. Going lower (à la Stardew/RF5) only works if you have a strong narrative spine to fill the time; going higher (Factorio) only works if the loop *is* crafting and the player is self-motivated.

### Time split: craft vs explore — typical ratios and variance

| Game | Craft % | Explore/gather/combat % | Driver |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stardew Valley | 0% | 100% | Heavy narrative onboarding |
| Rune Factory 5 | 0% | 100% (mostly cutscene) | JRPG-style story-first |
| Moonlighter | 0% | 100% | Combat-tutorial-first |
| Minecraft | 20–25% | 75–80% | Player-driven, no tutorial |
| Terraria | 25% | 75% | Guide NPC nudges, day/night pressure |
| Valheim | 30% | 70% | Hugin paces explicitly |
| Dwarf Fortress | ~10% (designate) | ~90% (plan/look) | Manager perspective |
| Satisfactory | 40% | 60% | Explicit HUB tutorial gates progress |
| Factorio | 50% | 50% | The loop *is* crafting |

**Median: ~25–30% craft, ~70–75% explore/gather.** Variance is driven by two axes:

- **Tutorial style**: explicit tutorial → more craft time (Satisfactory, Factorio). Open-world drop-in → more explore time (Minecraft, Valheim, Terraria).
- **Genre overlay**: JRPG/narrative skin (RF5, Stardew, Moonlighter) → ~0% craft in the first 5 min because dialogue and cutscenes consume the budget.

**Recommendation for Pockets**: if you want a *crafting-feeling* opening, aim for **~30–40% craft / ~60–70% explore-pick up** in the first 5 minutes. That puts you in the Valheim/Satisfactory band — heavy enough that the player feels the crafting identity, light enough that the world feels real and worth exploring. If Pockets has a narrative spine, you can compress crafting closer to 20% without breaking precedent — but going to 0% only works if you replace it with dialogue/NPC time, not dead air.

### Opinionated takeaways for Pockets

1. **Introduce in this order**: move → pick up → inventory → hit → personal-craft → place facility → station-craft. Don't reorder this without a reason; every successful game in the set follows it.
2. **Cap unique item types in the bag at ~6–8** by minute 5. More than 10 is overwhelming; fewer than 4 feels empty.
3. **One station is enough**. Two starts to fragment attention. Satisfactory and Factorio get away with two because they're explicitly about industrial scaling — that's a thesis, not a default.
4. **2–4 recipes** is the sweet spot. Minecraft proves you can teach the whole crafting paradigm in 4 recipes (planks, table, sticks, pickaxe).
5. **30/70 craft/explore** is the safe target ratio. Adjust toward 50/50 only if Pockets' identity is heavily craft-forward; toward 10/90 only if you have narrative or combat content to fill the gap.
