# First-5-Minutes Pacing Research — Extension Set 2

Applying prior methodology: **active gameplay only, cutscene/dialogue auto-advance time excluded**. The 5-minute budget begins when control is genuinely returned to the player.

---

## 1. World of Warcraft (Retail) — Exile's Reach

**Opening cutscene:** ~45s airship flyover → shipwreck → control granted on beach.

### Verbs and targets (first 5 active minutes)
- **Move** — beach → Captain Garrick.
- **Attack (auto + 1 ability)** — Quilboar Tideclaws (3–4 mobs).
- **Loot** (right-click corpses, AoE loot popup).
- **Talk / accept quest** ("Stand Your Ground", "Finding the Lost").
- **Heal NPC** via interact ("Murky") and/or pick up bandages.
- **Equip** — first weapon/shoulder upgrade swapped from bags via right-click.

### First 3–5 loot/crafting interactions
WoW has no crafting in Exile's Reach opening — it's pure combat-loot. First five drops typically:
1. **Linen Cloth** (gray/white trash, stackable).
2. **Tattered Cloth Vest** or class-appropriate armor token (green from quest reward).
3. **Quilboar Tusk** (quest item, "Finding the Lost" or similar).
4. **Broken Tideclaw** (gray vendor trash, ~5c).
5. **Quest weapon** — Captain Garrick hands out a class-appropriate 1H/2H by minute ~3.

### Mechanic-unveil order
1. Movement + camera (0:00).
2. Basic attack + first class ability hotkey (0:30 — popup tutorial).
3. Quest pickup/turn-in flow (1:00).
4. Looting + AoE loot bag (1:30).
5. Equipment compare tooltip + right-click equip (2:30).
6. Health/healing via bandage or quest NPC (3:30).
7. Group quest signpost + minimap tracking (4:30).

### Inventory at 5 min
- **Starting:** 16-slot backpack, four empty bag slots.
- **Filled by 5 min:** typically **6–9 unique types**, **8–12 stacks** counting starter Hearthstone, food/drink (2 stacks), class reagent if applicable (Warlock soul shards bag), quest weapon, 2–3 vendor trash grays, 1–2 quest items, 1 cloth stack.
- **Bag pressure:** none yet at 5 min — backpack ~50% full. Pressure typically kicks in around minute 20–25 when the first vendor becomes available at the base camp.

### Ceiling of unique types in 5 active minutes
**~10–12 unique types** is realistic if the player kills every mob path on the way to Garrick. Hard ceiling ~15 if RNG drops greens.

### Craft vs explore vs combat-loot ratio
**Craft 0% / Explore ~25% / Combat-loot ~75%.** WoW's opening is unambiguously combat-driven; exploration is rail-guided down a single beach corridor.

## 2. Final Fantasy XIV — A Realm Reborn opening

**Cutscene budget:** Opening carriage/airship ride + city arrival cinematic = **3.5–5 min of pure cutscene** depending on starting city (Limsa is shortest, Gridania longest). Clock starts when player exits the opening cinematic and gains movement in the starting plaza.

### Verbs and targets (first 5 active minutes, post-cutscene)
- **Move** — plaza navigation to class guild (Marauders' Guild / Gladiators' Guild / etc.).
- **Talk** to ~4–6 NPCs (queue triangle markers).
- **Accept class quest** + first **Hunting Log** / "kill 3 Aldgoats" style FATE-adjacent quest.
- **Combat** — 1–2 trash mobs outside city gates with a single weaponskill on cooldown.
- **Loot** — auto-loot to inventory (no corpse-click needed).

### First 3–5 loot/crafting interactions
1. **Weathered Axe / Sword / Wand** — gifted on class quest acceptance.
2. **Cracked Shell** or **Aldgoat Horn** (quest item).
3. **Bronze ingot / Cotton cloth** — typical FATE/quest reward at L1–3.
4. **Gil purse** (~50–100g) from first quest turn-in.
5. **Allagan Bronze Piece** (currency / vendor token, depending on patch).

Crafting is gated behind separate Disciple of Hand class quests — not encountered in the first 5 min unless the player explicitly chooses a crafter as starting class (rare).

### Mechanic-unveil order
1. Movement + sprint stamina (0:00, sprint introduced via tooltip in starting plaza).
2. NPC quest markers & journal (0:30).
3. Class guild registration → class identity (1:30).
4. Hotbar / weaponskill rotation popup (2:30).
5. First combat — target lock, GCD rhythm (3:00).
6. Auto-loot + inventory open (3:30).
7. Hunting Log / FATE pin on map (4:30).

### Inventory at 5 min
- **Starting inventory:** **140 slots** (FFXIV's player inventory is enormous — 35 slots × 4 tabs, plus separate Armory Chest with 25 slots per gear type).
- **Filled by 5 min:** **5–8 unique types** (starter gear set already pre-equipped, plus class weapon, 1–2 quest items, gil, food buff item, linkshell tutorial item).
- **Bag pressure:** **functionally zero in early game.** FFXIV separates gear (Armory Chest) from consumables (main inventory), so you can't fill up by accident. First real pressure is usually 30+ hours in, when retainers and crafting mats stack up.

### Ceiling of unique types in 5 active minutes
**~10 unique types**, capped by how few mobs the player can engage before the 5-min mark — most of minute 1–3 is NPC chatter even if you spam-skip.

### Craft vs explore vs combat-loot ratio
**Craft 0% / Explore ~60% / Combat-loot ~40%.** FFXIV's opening is exploration-heavy (navigating Limsa/Gridania/Ul'dah's confusing geometry), with combat as a late-arriving sideshow.

## 4. Arknights: Endfield

**Opening cutscene:** ~2 min establishing shot + Endministrator awakening. Control returned in a corridor/training-area sequence.

### Verbs and targets (first 5 active minutes)
- **Move** (3D third-person, WASD + camera).
- **Basic attack** with first operator (auto-targeting).
- **Switch operator** (squad of 2–4 in tutorial).
- **Dodge / use skill** (one-button skill on cooldown).
- **Interact** with first node/terminal.
- **Open map / quest journal**.

### First 3–5 loot/crafting interactions
1. **Originium shard** (currency-adjacent drop from first mob group).
2. **Scrap metal** or **basic ore** from a mining node (mining is core to Endfield's base loop).
3. **Tutorial gear chip** — operator skill upgrade material.
4. **Recipe unlock** — basic conveyor/processor blueprint introduced near minute 4–5 as the base-building tutorial onboards.
5. **First placed structure** — an extractor/drill on a resource node, demonstrating the factory loop.

### Mechanic-unveil order
1. Movement + camera (0:00).
2. Basic attack auto-lock (0:30).
3. Squad swap (1:00).
4. Skill button + cooldown (1:30).
5. Mining/harvest interact (2:30).
6. Base/factory placement UI (3:30 — this is the genre's headline feature).
7. Conveyor/automation hint (4:30).

### Inventory at 5 min
- **~6–10 unique types**: 2 currencies, 2 ores, 1 gear chip, 1 blueprint, 1–2 consumables, 1 quest item.
- **Bag pressure:** none — gacha-MMO inventories are deliberately oversized. Storage UI appears as part of base tutorial, but as a *feature* not a *pressure point*.

### Ceiling of unique types
**~12 unique types** in 5 active minutes if the player completes the mining tutorial.

### Craft vs explore vs combat-loot ratio
**Craft ~30% / Explore ~25% / Combat-loot ~45%.** Endfield is unusually crafting-forward for an action game in its first 5 minutes — the factory layer is front-loaded by design.

### Bag-pressure section (user's headline ask)

| Game | First bag-pressure moment | First solution introduced |
|---|---|---|
| **WoW** | ~min 20–25 (after first quest hub clears, backpack hits ~14/16) | **Vendor NPC** at Exile's Reach base camp (~min 20); first **extra bag drop** typically ~level 5–8 in zone 2 (well past 5 min mark) |

**Loot-diversity rate (new types per active minute):**
- WoW: ~2.0/min (highest in this set)
- FFXIV: ~1.2/min
- Pokopia: ~1.5/min (inferred)
- Endfield: ~1.6/min
- Compare to survival-craft median: ~1.0/min

**Key insight:** WoW's "constant loot diversity" claim is **mechanically real** — it's the only game in this set that combines a small starting bag (16 slots) with a fast diversity rate (~2 types/min) and grey vendor-trash that *forces* a vendor visit. FFXIV deliberately engineered this pressure *out* of its design (huge inventory + separated armory). They represent **opposite philosophical responses to the same loot-volume problem**.

### Implications for Pockets (the new design choice)

Pockets' entire premise — **everything exists inside nested bags** — means bag pressure isn't a side-effect but the **core mechanic**. The WoW vs FFXIV polarity directly maps onto the design space:

- **WoW-philosophy Pockets:** small starter bag, frequent type-diversity, vendor-trash sub-currency, forced engagement with sorting/discarding/upgrading bag. Crafting better bags becomes a treadmill goal. Matches `design/bag-crafting.md` aspirations.
- **FFXIV-philosophy Pockets:** large starter capacity with categorical separation (gear vs mats vs quest). Bag is a comfort feature, not a puzzle. Diminishes the "everything is a bag" identity.

The WoW model is the one that *justifies* Pockets' premise. The 2.0 new-types/min rate is the design target — it's fast enough to feel rich but slow enough that a 16-slot starter bag fills meaningfully in 5–10 minutes, generating the first "what do I throw out / where does this go" decision that's the game's core question.
